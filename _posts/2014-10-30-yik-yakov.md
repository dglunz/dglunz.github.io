[Modern information theory](https://www.khanacademy.org/computing/computer-science/informationtheory) recently sparked my interest in artificial intelligence. More specifically, I was curious to see if a bot could blend in and gain 'popularity' among an online audience. *Examples*

A few days ago, a friend introduced me to [Yik Yak](https://yikyakapp.com). For those of you living outside a college town, imagine Twitter and Reddit drunkenly had a one-night stand then proceeded to raise that mistaken offspring until it was the most popular kid in school. Oh, and it's completely anonymous. Which makes it the perfect playground for my newfound interest in AI.

Given that markov chains are memoryless (read: each word only lives in the present) they can produce a yak with the equivalent coherence of an inebriated college freshman (Yik Yak's demographic).

For hassle-free markov chains, I utilized the [marky markov](https://github.com/zolrath/marky_markov) (and the funky sentences) gem. All it takes to get started is a data set and a few lines of code:

{% highlight ruby %}
require 'marky_markov'

markov = MarkyMarkov::TemporaryDictionary.new
file_pattern = File.expand_path "../yaks.txt" , __FILE__

markov.parse_file file_pattern
puts markov.generate_n_sentences 1
markov.clear!
{% endhighlight %}

Any documentation on Yik Yak's API is currently nonexistant. I initially resorted to scraping the [Yik Yak homepage](www.yikyakapp.com), which contains 200 of the top yaks. The first iteration had a fairly small data set to work with but was broad enough to still be interesting.

 > I look to the fucking library to get your goddamned life together already. Sometimes the moon is 252,088 miles or two cvs receipts.

Despite making no sense at all, I decided to post a few of these.

 > Hint: don't wear your letters when you're passing a duck inbetween ponds hoping it doesn't attack you.

The results were even more awesome than the mental image of frat stars passing around an angry duck.

They even got some basic responses.

 With some help from [@marycutrali](https://twitter.com/marycutrali) I pulled a bit of json from   ```https://yikyakapp.com/api/getGreatest```
 and got another 300 or so Yaks to add to the markov dictionary.

Shit just kept getting weirder.

 > Was emailing my professor on my pancake. Thought I woke up to a huge clap of thunder, and then he tripped on a date and said yes, I hope today rocks

 I eventually found a [python wrapper](https://github.com/djtech42/YikYakTerminal) for the API, allowing me to pull in more relevant local data. Equally as nonsensical, but the local references made it slightly more believable.
